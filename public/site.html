About Prasanth

From Engineer to Advocate — A 20-Year Odyssey

Most advocates walk a straight line from law school to practice. Prasanth Raju walked a different path — and that path is precisely what makes him exceptional.

After graduating as an Electrical & Electronics Engineer from S.V. University, Prasanth went on to earn his MBA (PGDM) in Strategy and Finance from IIM Bangalore — one of Asia's most competitive business schools. A semester at McGill's Desautels Faculty of Management in Montreal sharpened his global lens.

He spent 12+ years navigating the worlds of technology, venture capital, and business strategy — building the kind of commercial intuition that most litigators never acquire. He was selected by IIM Bangalore to personally interact with Warren Buffett, attended the Harvard Project for Asian and International Relations (HPAIR) in Seoul, and was among the top 100 global students at the South American Business Forum in Buenos Aires.

When he transitioned to law — earning his LLB from the prestigious Government Law College, Mumbai and enrolling with the Bar Council of Maharashtra and Goa in 2022 — he brought an entirely new toolkit to the courtroom. Today he practises at the Bombay High Court and Supreme Court of India, bringing a strategist's mind to every dispute.

His April 2026 article in the Oxford Business Law Blog on AI and attorney-client privilege reflects his continuing mission: to be a lawyer for a complex, technology-driven world.
